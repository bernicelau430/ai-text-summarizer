- RSA Security reports that people are more aware of online security issues but lack confidence in companies' efforts.
- A poll showed almost 70% feel net firms aren't doing enough to protect people.
- RSA has developed a method using bank card chips and a special reader to generate unique, time-sensitive codes.
- This method aims to improve authentication by requiring a passcode from the card reader.
- RSA survey found 80% are fed up with passwords and want better login methods.
- Security companies are developing alternatives to passwords, like biometric measurements or smartcards.
- 43% of people are unwilling to provide personal information online.
- Ensuring extra security measures are in place protects companies as well as individuals.
