- British TV viewers are illegally downloading US shows from the net.
- 18% of downloaders are from the UK, and TV programme downloads have increased by 150% in the last year.
- A typical episode of 24 was downloaded by about 100,000 people globally, with 20,000 from the UK.
- It is now as easy to download a pirate TV show as it is to programme a VCR.
- The increased number of broadband connections helps aid quick downloads.
- It is thought that about 80,000 to 100,000 people in the UK download TV programmes.
- People can sometimes watch new episodes in Britain before US audiences.
- There are programs that automatically search and store TV programmes.
- The TV industry should consider offering a legal way to download shows.
