- The latest version of the net browser can be controlled by voice command and will read pages aloud.
- Unlike commercially available voice recognition software, Opera doesn't have to be trained.
- Features like voice activation are not solely aimed at visually impaired people.
- The voice recognition function is currently only available in English.
- The browser doesn't yet work well with screen reader software, so its accessibility features are more likely to appeal to those with some residual vision.
- The voice features are based on IBM technology and are currently only available in the Windows version.
- Around 50 voice commands are available, and users will have to wear a headset with a microphone.
