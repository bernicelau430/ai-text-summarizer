- Consumers are concerned about the use of RFID tags in shops, a survey says
- 55% of respondents are concerned about tracking consumers via product purchases
- 59% are worried RFID tags will allow data to be used more freely by third parties
- 52% believe RFID tags can be read from a distance
- Consumers would accept RFID if it could reduce car theft or speed recovery of stolen items
- Retailers need to inform and educate people about RFID before it's accepted
- Awareness of tags among consumers in Europe is low
