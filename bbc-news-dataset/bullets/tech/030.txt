- The Legal Affairs Committee (JURI) suggests that the Commission resubmit the Computer Implemented Inventions Directive.
- MEPs failed to back the original directive.
- Opponents welcomed the decision, hoping for fuller debates about the implications.
- The directive aims to offer patent protection to inventions using software.
- Supporters say current laws are inefficient and the directive would create a level playing field.
- Critics argue that it could favor large firms over small ones and impact open-source software innovation.
