- An organization has been launched to encourage disabled people to get involved in all aspects of motorsport
- Motorcycle racer Roy Tansley developed an electronic sequential gear changer after an accident that resulted in the amputation of part of his left leg
- Another exhibit on the Motorsport Endeavour stand is a Subaru Impreza rally car adapted to accommodate various disabilities
- One customer had all four limbs amputated
- Tansley's invention, the Pro-Shift, works with Hewland gearboxes commonly used in motorcycle racing
- The Pro-Shift saves at least 20 seconds per lap when he competes in the Isle of Man TT
