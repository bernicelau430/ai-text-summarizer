- Mr Vetham said the majority of people surveyed (52%) believed that RFID tags could be read from a distance.
- Fifty-nine percent of people said they were worried that RFID tags would allow data to be used more freely by third parties.
- Mr. Vetham said the survey showed that retailers needed to inform and educate people about RFID before it would become accepted technology.
- Over half (55%) of the respondents said they were either concerned or very concerned that RFID tags would allow businesses to track consumers via product purchases.
- At least one consumer group has claimed that RFID chips could be used to secretly identify people and the things they are carrying or wearing.
- The survey showed that awareness of tags among consumers in Europe was low.
