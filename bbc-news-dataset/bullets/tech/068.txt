- Jef Raskin, head of the team behind the first Macintosh computer, has died.
- Raskin was one of the first employees at Apple.
- He made many design decisions that made the Mac distinctive.
- Raskin was put in charge of a team to design a cheap, user-friendly computer aimed at consumers.
- The Macintosh was named after Raskin's favorite apple.
- Raskin left Apple in 1982.
- Raskin was diagnosed with pancreatic cancer in December 2004 and died on 26 February.
