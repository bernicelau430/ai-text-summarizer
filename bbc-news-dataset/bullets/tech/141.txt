- The US has taken the lead in supercomputing with IBM's Blue Gene/L machine, reaching 42.7 teraflops in October.
- Since 1976, computational speed has increased 500,000 times.
- The Blue Gene/L machine is expected to be five million times faster upon completion next year.
- Silicon Graphics' Columbia supercomputer is in second place.
- The Earth Simulator, previously in first place since June 2002, is now third.
- IBM test results show that Blue Gene/L has managed speeds of 70.72 teraflops.
