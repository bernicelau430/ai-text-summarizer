- The Motorsport Endeavour Club encourages disabled people to get involved in all aspects of motorsport
- They have technologies on display at the Autosport International 2005 show
- Roy Tansley developed an electronic sequential gear changer (Pro-Shift) after a leg amputation
- Pro-Shift is designed for Hewland gearboxes
- A Subaru Impreza rally car has been adapted to accommodate a variety of disabilities
- The car on display is fitted with a 'duck clutch'
- Customers include arm amputees, quadriplegics, stroke survivors, and a woman with all four limbs amputated
- Mr. Tansley reckons that the Pro-Shift saves at least 20 seconds per lap when he competes in the Isle of Man TT
