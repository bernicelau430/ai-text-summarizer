- The first convictions for piracy over peer-to-peer networks have been handed down in the US.
- Two men operated central hubs in a piracy community organized across the Direct Connect peer-to-peer network.
- The Department of Justice said both men pleaded guilty to one count of conspiracy to commit felony copyright infringement.
- Membership required users to share between one and 100 gigabytes of files.
- The FBI conducted raids on suspected pirates.
