- The first convictions for piracy over peer-to-peer networks have been handed down in the US
- Two men pleaded guilty to one count of conspiracy to commit felony copyright infringement
- They operated central hubs in a piracy community organized across the Direct Connect peer-to-peer network
- They faced charges following raids in August on suspected pirates by the FBI
- Five raids were on homes, and one on a net service firm
- The piracy group called itself the Underground Network
- Membership required sharing between 1 and 100 gigabytes of files
