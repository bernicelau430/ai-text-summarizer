- Sony, IBM, and Toshiba will unveil the Cell processor at a technology conference.
- The chip has been worked on for three years.
- It is designed for use in graphics workstations, the new PlayStation console.
- The chip has been described as a supercomputer on a chip.
- The Cell processor could bridge the gap between movies and video games.
- IBM will start producing the chip in early 2005.
- The chip can handle detailed graphics and the data demands of films and broadband media.
- The chip is reported to be up to 10 times faster than current processors.
- Details of the chip will be released at the International Solid State Circuits Conference in San Francisco.
