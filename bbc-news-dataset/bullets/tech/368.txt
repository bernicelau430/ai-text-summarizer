- Goal: Help net service firms communicate and push attacks back to their source.
- Attacks involve five to ten gigabytes of traffic.
- Arbor Networks created the fingerprinting system.
- Signatures of attacks will be shared with those affected.
- Arbor is providing the service and fingerprint data for free.
- Information will be passed down the chain of networks to tackle the problem.
- Firms believe the system will help trace attacks to their source.
- Eighty large net service firms have implemented software to automatically spot and stop attacks.
- Criminals use swarms of remotely controlled computers for denial of service attacks, worms, and spam.
