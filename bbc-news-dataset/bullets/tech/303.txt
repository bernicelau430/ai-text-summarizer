- The US Department of Justice said two men operated central hubs in a piracy community on the Direct Connect peer-to-peer network.
- Both men pleaded guilty to one count of conspiracy to commit felony copyright infringement.
- The two men faced charges following raids in August on suspected pirates by the FBI.
- The piracy group called itself the Underground Network, and membership required users to share between 1 and 100 gigabytes of files.
- These are the first convictions for piracy over peer-to-peer networks in the US.
- Five raids were on the homes of suspected copyright thieves and one on a net service firm.
