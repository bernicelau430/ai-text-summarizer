- Sonaptic is developing more efficient 3D audio technology for small consumer devices like mobile phones.
- A fishing game is the first title to use the technology, creating a 3D sound field.
- Sonaptic is working with NTT DoCoMo to set standards for 3D audio on mobile phones.
- The technology synthesizes information the brain uses to understand sound direction on a mobile phone handset.
- Firms AM3D and SRS offer stereo-widening technology for mobile phones.
- Sonaptic hopes the technology will have a big impact in mobile gaming and music downloading.
- Japan was chosen as the initial focus due to its advanced mobile phone market.
- The surround sound demo is startling when emerging from a small mobile phone.
- The ear's structure works as a 3D encoder for sound, helping the brain understand sound origin.
