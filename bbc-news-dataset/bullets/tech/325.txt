- The first convictions for piracy over peer-to-peer networks have been handed down in the US.
- Two men pleaded guilty to conspiracy to commit felony copyright infringement.
- The two men operated the central hubs in a piracy community called the Underground Network.
- Membership of the network demanded users share between 1 and 100 gigabytes of files.
- The charges followed FBI raids in August on homes of suspected copyright thieves and a net service firm.
