- Consumers are very concerned about the use of radio frequency ID (RFID) tags in shops, a survey says
- Survey showed awareness of tags among consumers in Europe was low
- Survey also showed people would accept RFID if they felt that the technology could mean a reduction in car theft or faster recovery of stolen items
- More than half (55%) of the respondents said they were either concerned or very concerned that RFID tags would allow businesses to track consumers via product purchases
- 59% of people said they were worried that RFID tags would allow data to be used more freely by third parties
