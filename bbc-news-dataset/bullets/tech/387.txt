- Consumers are concerned about RFID tags in shops, according to a survey.
- 59% of people worry that RFID tags will allow data to be used freely by third parties.
- More than half (55%) are concerned RFID tags will let businesses track consumers via product purchases.
- At least one consumer group claims RFID chips could be used to secretly identify people and the things they are carrying or wearing.
- Awareness of RFID tags among consumers in Europe is low.
