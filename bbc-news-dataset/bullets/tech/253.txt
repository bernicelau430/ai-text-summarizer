- Lifts in Taipei 101 Tower are the world's fastest.
- Take 30 seconds to reach the top of the 508m tower.
- Can speed up to 24 passengers on a 382m track.
- Guinness Book of Records declared the 17m per second speed the swiftest.
- Lifts take longer to descend (almost a minute).
- 17m/s translates to about 38mph (61km/h).
- Technologies:
  - Pressure control system prevents "ear popping".
  - Active control system balances the lift and removes vibrations.
  - Streamlined cars reduce whistling noise.
- Toshiba Elevator and Building Systems installed the lifts.
