- The first convictions for piracy over peer-to-peer networks have been handed down in the US.
- Two men operated central hubs in a piracy community organized across the Direct Connect peer-to-peer network.
- Both men pleaded guilty to one count of conspiracy to commit felony copyright infringement.
- The piracy group called itself the Underground Network.
- Membership demanded users share between 1 and 100 gigabytes of files.
- The men faced charges following raids by the FBI.
