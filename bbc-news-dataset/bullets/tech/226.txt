- The digital divide will persist, with those without home access missing out more.
- Internet access on other devices is mainly for those who already have it.
- Optimistic predictions about convergence bridging the digital divide may be wrong.
- Organizations like BT have a responsibility to help tackle the problem.
- Grassroots activity addressing specific community needs is essential to overcome the digital divide.
- The idea that the digital divide will evaporate with time is "wishful thinking."
- Low-income individuals, the disabled, and the elderly will disproportionately make up the digital have-nots by 2025.
- As more tasks move online, the divide will become more burdensome for those without net access.
- BT's Everybody Online project offers internet access to people in deprived communities and has seen positive results.
