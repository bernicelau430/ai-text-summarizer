- An organization has been launched to encourage disabled people to get involved in all aspects of motorsport.
- Motorsport Endeavour Club hopes to get more disabled people involved in all areas of the sport.
- Roy Tansley developed his electronic sequential gear changer following an accident.
- He needed to find a way of changing gear using his left leg.
- Tansley's invention, the Pro-Shift, is designed to work with Hewland gearboxes used in motorcycle racing.
- A Subaru Impreza rally car has been adapted to accommodate a variety of disabilities.
- The car is fitted with a 'duck clutch' used instead of the clutch pedal.
- Tansley reckons that the Pro-Shift saves at least 20 seconds per lap when he competes in the Isle of Man TT.
