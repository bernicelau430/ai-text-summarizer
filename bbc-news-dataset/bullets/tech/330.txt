- The European Parliament has thrown out a bill that would have allowed software to be patented.
- Both the European Parliament and a qualified majority of EU states have to approve the bill to become law.
- The software patents bill could be substantially re-drafted or even scrapped during consultation.
- Politicians unanimously rejected the bill, requiring another round of consultation for it to have a chance of becoming law.
- Proponents of the bill said it was a good compromise that avoided the excesses of the American system.
- The benefits of the bill had been obscured by special interest groups.
