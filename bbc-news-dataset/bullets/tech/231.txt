- The games industry will consider ways to ensure parents know which video games are suitable for children.
- Violent games have faced controversy, such as Manhunt being blamed in a murder case.
- Trade and Industry Secretary Patricia Hewitt raised the issue of warnings on games for adults.
- Industry will consider how to make sure parents know what games their children should and shouldn't play.
- Possible measures include a campaign to explain that many games are made for an adult audience and changes to labeling.
- The issue was discussed at a meeting between UK government officials, industry representatives, and the British Board of Film Classification.
- Parents are expected to spend millions on video games and consoles this Christmas.
