- IBM is increasing its commitment to Linux software due to greater customer demand for open-source software.
- Linux and the open-source software movement are based on the premise that developers should be free to tinker with software programs.
- IBM is spending $100m over the next three years to boost Linux use.
- The money will fund technical, research, and marketing initiatives to help customers use Linux on various devices, from handheld computers and phones to powerful servers.
