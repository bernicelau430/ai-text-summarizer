- Consumers are very concerned about the use of radio frequency ID (RFID) tags in shops.
- 52% of people believe that RFID tags could be read from a distance.
- 59% are worried that RFID tags would allow data to be used more freely by third parties.
- More than half of the respondents are concerned that RFID tags would allow businesses to track consumers.
- Caspian has claimed that RFID chips could be used to secretly identify people.
- Awareness of tags among consumers in Europe was low.
- Retailers need to inform and educate people about RFID before it will become accepted technology.
