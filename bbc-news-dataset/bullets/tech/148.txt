- Apple is accused of unlawfully bundling its monopoly in the market for online digital music recordings and portable hard drive digital music players.
- Apple has sold more than six million iPods and has an 87% market share.
- Apple's online music store uses a different format for songs than competitors.
- A lawsuit states that Apple has turned an open standard into an artifice that prevents consumers from using the portable hard drive digital music player of their choice.
- Apple uses technology to ensure each song bought only plays on the iPod.
