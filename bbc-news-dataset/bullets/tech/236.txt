- Online pages from scanned books will not have ads but will have links to Amazon.
- The New York library allows Google to include a portion of books no longer covered by copyright.
- The full libraries of Michigan and Stanford universities, as well as archives at Harvard, Oxford and the New York Public Library are included.
- Oxford wants Google to scan books originally published in the 19th Century and held in the Bodleian Library.
- There will also be links to public libraries so books can be borrowed.
- Approximately one million books will be scanned by Google, less than 15% of the Bodleian's total collection.
- Harvard is limiting its participation to 40,000 books.
