- Survey: consumers concerned about RFID tags in shops.
- 52% surveyed believed RFID tags could be read from a distance.
- 55% concerned that RFID tags would allow businesses to track consumers via product purchases.
- 59% worried that RFID tags would allow data to be used more freely by third parties.
- Awareness of tags among consumers in Europe was low.
- People would accept RFID if they felt that the technology could mean a reduction in car theft or faster recovery of stolen items.
- Retailers need to inform and educate people about RFID before it would become accepted technology.
