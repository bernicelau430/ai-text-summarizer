- A new European directive could put software writers at risk of legal action.
- The proposed directive is supported by many of the major software companies.
- The results will be disastrous for small companies, innovative programmers and the free and open source software movement.
- The directive goes through it has to be written into national laws and then there will be a steady stream of legal actions against small companies and open source products.
- Much of the really useful software we use every day, programs like the Apache web server, the GNU/Linux operating system and the fearsomely popular Firefox browser, is developed outside company structures by people who do not have legal departments to check for patent infringements.
