- Any large scale data flood could knock out access to many more sites than just the one the criminals were targeting.
- It's about understanding what's genuine traffic and keeping attack traffic from going to the site.
- The most common types of attacks hit sites with 10 megabytes of data over short periods of time.
- Bigger attacks sending down 200 megabytes of traffic or more were rarely seen.
- Customers were getting hit every four to five days.
- Technology allows to detect attacks in minutes.
- The only way to properly combat these so-called Distributed Denial-of-Service attacks was with intelligent net-based systems.
- The serious players tend to precede an attack with some kind of ransom e-mail.
- DDoS attacks typically use thousands of computers in many different countries.
- Each participating machine only sends a small part of the entire data flood.
- Net firms were starting to work more closely together on the problem of DDoS attacks.
