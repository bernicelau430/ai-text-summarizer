- Consumers are concerned about the use of RFID tags in shops, a survey shows
- 55% of respondents were either concerned or very concerned that RFID tags would allow businesses to track consumers via product purchases
- 59% of people said they were worried that RFID tags would allow data to be used more freely by third parties
- More than half (52%) believed that RFID tags could be read from a distance
- The survey also showed people would accept RFID if they felt that the technology could mean a reduction in car theft or faster recovery of stolen items
- The survey showed that awareness of tags among consumers in Europe was low
