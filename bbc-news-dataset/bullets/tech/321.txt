- 52% of people surveyed believed RFID tags could be read from a distance.
- People would accept RFID if it meant a reduction in car theft or faster recovery of stolen items.
- 59% were worried that RFID tags would allow data to be used more freely by third parties.
- Retailers needed to inform and educate people about RFID before it would become accepted technology.
- 55% were concerned that RFID tags would allow businesses to track consumers.
- Consumers are very concerned about the use of RFID tags in shops.
- RFID chips could be used to secretly identify people and the things they are carrying or wearing.
- Awareness of tags among consumers in Europe was low.
