- High-definition TV (HDTV) is finally taking off, mainly in the US and Japan.
- HDTV offers up to 1,080 active lines, with each line made up of 1,920 pixels.
- British TV pictures are made up of 625 lines and about 700 pixels.
- Many high-end European TV programmes, such as the Athens Olympics, are already produced in high-definition.
- BSkyB is planning HDTV services in the UK in 2006.
- The BBC will start broadcasting in HDTV when the time is right, with a whole set of programming.
- Programmes need to be broadcast in HDTV format and you need an HDTV set to receive them.
- Viewers in Japan, the US, Australia, Canada, and South Korea are already embracing HDTV.
- The HDTV hype from the US and Japan has spurred European broadcasters and consumer electronics companies to push for change.
- Broadcasters like the BBC are working on their own HDTV plans, with no launch date in sight.
- All television images are made up of pixels and scan lines.
