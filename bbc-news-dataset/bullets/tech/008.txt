- Commodore was one of the pioneers in home computing
- The Commodore 64, launched in 1982, was one of the first affordable home PCs
- Commodore International filed for bankruptcy in 1994 and was sold to Dutch firm Tulip Computers
- The brand could be resurrected after being bought by a US-based digital music distributor
- The Commodore 64 sold more than any other single computer system, even to this day
- In the late 1980s, the firm was a great rival to Atari
