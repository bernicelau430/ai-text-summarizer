- Kostas Kenteris and Katerina Thanou will fight the provisional two-year bans imposed on them by the IAAF at an independent tribunal this weekend.
- The panel will also decide on the two-year ban imposed on the athletes' controversial coach Christos Tzekos by the IAAF.
- Kenteris and Thanou also face criminal charges in Greece for allegedly faking the motorcycle accident.
- The sprint duo face charges of failing to give information on their location and refusing to submit to a drugs test.
- They also missed tests in Tel Aviv, Chicago and Athens before the Games.
- Eight criminal charges have been laid against the sprinters as well as Tzekos, an eyewitness to the accident and hospital officials.
- Kenteris, and Thanou, quit the Olympics after failing to give samples on the eve of the opening ceremony.
