- Kenteris and Thanou face criminal charges in Greece for allegedly faking the motorcycle accident.
- They will fight the provisional two-year bans imposed on them by the IAAF at an independent tribunal this weekend.
- They face charges of failing to give information on their location and refusing to submit to a drugs test.
- Eight criminal charges have been laid against the sprinters as well as Tzekos, an eyewitness to the accident and hospital officials.
- Kenteris and Thanou quit the Olympics on 18 August after failing to give samples on the eve of the opening ceremony.
