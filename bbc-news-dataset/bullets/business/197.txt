- Turkey's State Planning Organisation says if admitted into the EU, Turkey would contribute almost 6bn euros to its budget by 2014.
- GDP is set to grow by 6% per year on average; its contribution would rise from less than 5bn euros in 2014 to almost 9bn euros by 2020.
- Tourism industry is booming and revenues from visitors should more than double to $21bn in three years.
- A key factor for EU decision makers should be whether or not Turkey has met its economic criteria.
- Turkey's massive debt - which includes $23bn owed to the IMF - remains a major obstacle to its ambition of joining the EU.
- Costs are projected to be vast during the early years of Turkey's membership, with subsidies alone estimated to exceed 16.5bn euros.
