- Job creation was one of last year's main concerns for the US economy.
- The US created fewer jobs than expected in December.
- Analysts said that the dip in hiring was not enough to derail the world's biggest economy.
- The conditions are set for steady growth in 2005.
- The Fed lifted borrowing costs five times last year to 2.25%, citing evidence the US economic recovery was becoming more robust.
- The unemployment rate stayed at 5.4% in December, and about 200,000 jobs will need to be created each month if that figure is to drop.
