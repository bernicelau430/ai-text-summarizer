- Bob Murray, Sunderland FC chairman, launched a £1.5m bid for the club.
- Murray agreed to buy BSkyB's 4.76% holding in the club, taking his stake to 42.3%.
- Murray must offer 31p a share for the remaining shares he doesn't own.
- Murray expects to pay a maximum of £1.53m for the remaining shares if the offer is fully accepted.
- Murray hopes to take full control of the Championship side.
- Murray believes a single majority owner will make it easier to attract investment.
- Murray stated that the football sector is experiencing significant changes and uncertainty.
