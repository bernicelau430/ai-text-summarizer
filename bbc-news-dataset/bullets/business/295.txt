- Business confidence among Japanese manufacturers has weakened for the first time since March 2003.
- The economy is at a pause but unlikely to fall.
- Confidence among non-manufacturers was unchanged in the final quarter of 2004, but it is forecast to drop one point in the March survey.
- It will feel a bit slower next year than this year, and growth may be a bit more gentle but the situation is that the recovery will continue.
- December's confidence level was below that seen in September.
- Companies reported they expect to invest 7.7% more in the year to March 2005 than the previous year - up from expectations of 6.1% increase in the September Tankan.
