- 100,000 people are expected to converge on Porto Alegre for the World Social Forum.
- The World Social Forum is the so-called "anti-Davos" for campaigners against globalisation and for fair trade.
- PricewaterhouseCoopers survey: four in ten business leaders were "very confident" that their companies would see sales rise in 2005.
- More than 20 world leaders are expected to attend the Davos forum including Tony Blair and Jacques Chirac.
- WHO: 700,000 people in poor countries were on life-extending drugs - up from 440,000 six months earlier but amounting to only 12% of those who needed them.
- Bono and Angelina Jolie will also put in an appearance at the Davos forum.
- Davos forum is dominated by business issues - from outsourcing to corporate leadership.
- Climate change and the fight against Aids are leading the list of concerns for the first day of the World Economic Forum.
