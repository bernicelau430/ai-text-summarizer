- US Airways, the seventh-largest carrier in the US, filed for bankruptcy protection for the second time in two years last September.
- A union representing 5,200 US Airways flight attendants agreed to a new contract that cuts pay by nearly 10%.
- The deal will help the carrier save about $94m as it tries to cut costs by nearly $1bn a year.
- US Airways management has said it may need to start liquidating assets if it does not receive concessions from all staff by the middle of this month.
