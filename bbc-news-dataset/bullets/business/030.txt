- US retail sales fell 0.3% in January, the biggest decline since August, driven by a fall in car sales.
- Excluding the car sector, US retail sales were up 0.6%, twice what some analysts had been expecting.
- The 3.3% fall in car sales had been expected, coming after December's 4% rise.
- US retail spending is expected to rise in 2005, but not as quickly as in 2004.
