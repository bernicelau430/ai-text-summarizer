- Venezuelan authorities will seize land owned by a British company.
- The seizure is part of President Chavez's agrarian reform program.
- The Vestey Group has owned the land since 1920.
- The Vestey Group will co-operate fully with the authorities.
- Officials in Cojedes state said that farmland owned by a subsidiary of the Vestey Group would be taken and used to settle poor farmers.
- The firm's Agroflora subsidiary operates 13 farms in Venezuela and insists that it has complied fully with Venezuelan law.
- The Vestey Group said it had not been informed of any planned seizure.
- Last month, the government said it had identified more than 500 idle farms and had yet to consider the status of a further 40,000.
- The authorities said landowners whose titles were in order and whose farms were productive had "nothing to fear".
- Under President Chavez, the Venezuelan government has steadily expanded the state's involvement in the country's economy.
