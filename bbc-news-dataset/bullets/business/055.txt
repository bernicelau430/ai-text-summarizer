- The South African government has put tax cuts and increased social spending at the centre of its latest budget.
- The South African economy had grown by an average of 3.2% over the past four years.
- The focus of the 2005 budget was more for all.
- Those earning less than 35,000 rand a year will be exempt from income tax.
- The extra 22.3bn rand in social spending will be partly met by higher fuel, tobacco and alcohol taxes.
- Economist Colen Garrow said the budget looked as if it would stimulate economic growth.
