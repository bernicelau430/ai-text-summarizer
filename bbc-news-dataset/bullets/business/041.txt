- Alan Greenspan warned that allowing huge US budget deficits could have severe consequences.
- He urged Congress to reinstate lapsed rules that require tax cuts and spending to be offset elsewhere in the budget.
- The US economy is growing at a reasonably good pace, but budget concerns are clouding the economic outlook.
- Raising taxes to narrow the deficit gap could pose a significant risk to the economy by dampening growth and spending.
- He urged Congress to take action to cut the deficit, such as increasing taxes.
- As US growth gathers steam and incomes rise that should lead to a narrowing of the deficit.
