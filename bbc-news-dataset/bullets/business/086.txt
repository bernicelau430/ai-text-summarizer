- UK manufacturing grew at its slowest pace in one-and-a-half years in January, according to a survey.
- The CIPS purchasing manager index (PMI) fell to 51.8 from 53.3 in December.
- The survey is more upbeat than official figures suggesting a manufacturing recession.
- Analysts said the manufacturing recovery was running out of steam.
- The strong pound has dented exports while rising oil and metals prices kept costs high.
