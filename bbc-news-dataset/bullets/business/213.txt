- Alan Greenspan gave a speech at a Scottish church in honor of Adam Smith.
- The speech was the 14th Adam Smith Lecture in Kirkcaldy, Fife.
- Greenspan compared Smith to Mozart and said the world could never repay its debt to Smith.
- Greenspan said Smith's philosophy on self-interest, laissez-faire economics, and competition had been a force for good.
- Gordon Brown introduced Greenspan as the world's greatest economist.
- The Adam Smith Lecture celebrates the author of 1776's Wealth of Nations.
