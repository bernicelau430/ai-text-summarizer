- Turkey aims to join the EU and is trying to show it will be an asset, not a burden.
- Turkey claims its economy is improving, with GDP set to grow by 6% per year.
- It claims it would contribute billions to the EU budget.
- Critics say Turkey's debt and current account deficit are major obstacles.
- The IMF says Turkey's debt is double that of new EU member states.
- Some predict high initial expenses for the EU if Turkey joins.
- The final decision will be political.
