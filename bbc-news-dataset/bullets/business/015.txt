- Passengers must be compensated if flights are cancelled or delayed for over two hours due to the airline's fault.
- Airlines are exempt from compensation in "extraordinary circumstances".
- The new EU regulation aims to deter airlines from overbooking flights by increasing passenger compensation.
- EU-based airlines and flights departing from the EU must comply with the new compensation rules.
- Compensation is required when passengers are denied boarding against their will.
- Previously, only scheduled flight operators compensated for overbooking, not cancellations.
- Low-cost airlines are concerned that compensation could exceed ticket value.
- The EC has initiated an information campaign to educate passengers on their new rights.
- New technology enables airlines to operate in bad weather more easily.
