- BMW announced £100m of new investment in the Mini, less than four years after its launch.
- Output is impressive compared to the Rover factory in Longbridge.
- Last year, almost one in six cars sold by BMW was a Mini.
- When launched, the cheapest Mini cost just more than £10,000, now buyers will have to fork out almost £11,500.
- BMW's Mini adventure has made good much of what went wrong during its stewardship of the UK car maker Rover, which it sold for £10 five years ago.
