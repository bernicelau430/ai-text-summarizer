- Italy's Illva Saronno has agreed to buy 33% of Changyu, the largest wine maker in China.
- The Italian liqueur maker will acquire the shares from the Yantai State Asset Management Bureau.
- Yantai State Asset Management Bureau owns 55% of Changyu.
- Chinese wine sales are growing, with wine sales in 2003 up 25% at 61.1bn yuan.
- The consumption of wine in China is still low, at just 0.22 litres per capita.
