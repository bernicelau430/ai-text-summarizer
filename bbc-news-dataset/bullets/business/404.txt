- Continental Airlines warned it could run out of cash if it fails to reduce wage costs by $500m by the end of February.
- The airline expects to lose "hundreds of millions of dollars" in 2005 if it does not make any cuts.
- The firm also announced changes to wage and benefits for most of its US-based management and clerical staff, which would shave a further $48m a year from its costs.
- Shares in Continental Airlines have tumbled after the firm's warning.
