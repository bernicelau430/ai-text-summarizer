- Chile's copper industry registered record earnings of $14.2bn in 2004.
- The copper price reached a 16-year high in October 2004.
- Chile, the biggest copper producer in the world, is now analyzing ways of to keep prices stable at their current high levels.
- Cochilco expects for 2005 a slight reduction on copper prices and forecasts export earnings will fall 10.7%.
- Strong demand from China's fast-growing economy and high prices have fueled production.
