- US oil prices have fallen by 6%, driven down by forecasts of a mild winter in the Northeast.
- Statistics showed that stockpiles of oil products in the US had risen, indicating fewer supply disruptions this winter.
- Oil prices have broken records in 2004, topping $50 a barrel due to unrest in Iraq and Saudi Arabia, rising demand, and supply bottlenecks.
- Light crude oil futures fell $2.86 to $41.32 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange (Nymex).
- US crude is still 30% more expensive than at the beginning of 2004, boosted by growing demand and refinery bottlenecks.
