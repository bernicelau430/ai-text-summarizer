- Germany's economy shrank 0.2% in the last three months of 2004, upsetting hopes of a sustained recovery.
- Growth in the third quarter had been zero, putting the economy at a standstill from July onward.
- Figures confounded hopes of a 0.2% expansion in the fourth quarter in Europe's biggest economy.
- Germany has been reliant on exports to get its economy back on track.
  - Unemployment of more than five million and impending cuts to welfare mean German consumers have kept their money to themselves.
- The ECB's mandate is to fight rising prices by boosting interest rates.
  - That could further threaten Germany's hopes of recovery.
