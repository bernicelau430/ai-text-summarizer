- Sri Lanka's president has launched a $3.5bn reconstruction drive, appealing for peace and national unity.
- The IMF said damage from the tsunami would cut one percentage point from Sri Lanka's economic growth this year.
- The Sri Lankan authorities estimate the physical damage at $1.3 to $1.5bn.
- The ILO estimates that 400,000 Sri Lankans have lost their jobs, mostly in tourism, fisheries, and agriculture.
- Earnings from tourism this year are expected to be 15% lower than last year.
- The IMF approved Sri Lanka's request for a freeze on loan repayments.
- President Kumaratunga said it was important to find a peaceful solution to internal conflict.
- Reconstruction efforts in eastern Sri Lanka have been hampered by tensions between the two sides.
