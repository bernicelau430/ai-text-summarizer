- Delta Air Lines is cutting domestic fares by as much as 50% to ensure its financial survival.
- No one-way economy fare will be priced higher than $499.
- No first-class fare will be priced higher than $599.
- Shares of Delta, Continental, and American Airlines all fell by more than 7% on the announcement.
- Delta lost $646m in the three months to September and cut 6,900 jobs worldwide.
- The airline aims to slash $5bn from its costs.
- Delta is eliminating a Saturday-night stay requirement on discount fares.
- The airline will give further reductions to customers opting for non-refundable tickets, booking in advance, and online.
