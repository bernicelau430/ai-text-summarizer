- Reserve Bank of Australia raised interest rates 0.25% to 5.5%, the first increase in over a year.
- The Bank acted to curb inflation, but the move was criticized given signs of an economic slowdown.
- Data showed the Australian economy grew just 0.1% between October and December, and 1.5% annually.
- The government maintained the economy is strong with low unemployment.
- Analysts expressed concern about the strength of the economy despite the rate rise being factored in.
