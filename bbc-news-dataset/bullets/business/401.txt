- A Marsh executive admitted to instructing insurance companies to submit non-competitive bids between 2002 and 2004.
- Marsh was able to control the insurance market by determining which insurers won business from clients.
- This practice protected incumbent insurers and maximized Marsh's fees.
- The executive agreed to testify in future cases during the industry inquiry.
- Marsh & McLennan stated they were saddened by the development.
