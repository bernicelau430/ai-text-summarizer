- Open Society Institute (OSI), financed by George Soros, accuses Kazakhstan of trying to close its local office.
- OSI shut its Moscow office last year and has withdrawn from Uzbekistan and Belarus.
- Kazakhstan's president, Nursultan Abish-uly Nazarbayev, has dominated since the break up of the Soviet Union in 1991.
- Insulting the president and officials has been made a criminal offence.
- OSI claims the legal prosecution is an attempt to force Soros Foundation-Kazakhstan to cease activities.
- OSI was accused of helping to topple Georgia's former president.
