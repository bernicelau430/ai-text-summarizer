- President Bush presented his 2006 budget, cutting domestic spending to lower a record deficit projected to peak at $427bn this year
- Military spending is set to rise by 4.8%, to reach $419.3bn
- The budget does not include the cost of running military operations in Iraq and Afghanistan, for which the administration is expected to seek an extra $80bn from Congress later this year
- The State Department's planned budget would rise to just under $23bn, including almost $6bn to assist US allies in the "war on terror"
- The spending plan for the year beginning 1 October is banking on a healthy US economy to boost government income by 6.1% to $2.18 trillion
