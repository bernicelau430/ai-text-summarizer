- President Bush presented his 2006 budget, cutting domestic spending to lower a record deficit projected to peak at $427bn this year.
- Military spending is set to rise by 4.8%, to reach $419.3bn.
- The budget does not include the cost of running military operations in Iraq and Afghanistan.
- Bush has promised to halve the US's massive budget deficit within five years.
- The $2.58 trillion budget submitted to Congress affects 150 domestic programs from farming to the environment, education and health.
