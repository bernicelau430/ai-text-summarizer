- German Chancellor Gerhard Schroeder has called for radical reform of the EU's stability pact.
- He wants to grant countries more flexibility over their budget deficits.
- Mr. Schroeder said existing fiscal rules should be loosened to allow countries to run deficits above the current 3% limit if they met certain criteria.
- Exemptions were needed to take into account the cost of domestic reform programs and changing economic conditions.
- Mr. Schroeder also called for heads of government to play a larger role in shaping reforms to the pact.
- Deficits should be allowed to rise above 3% if countries meet several "mandatory criteria".
