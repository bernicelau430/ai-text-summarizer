- The Reserve Bank of Australia increased interest rates by 0.25% to 5.5%.
- This is the first upwards move in more than a year.
- Opposition parties and some analysts said the move was ill-timed.
- Data showed the Australian economy grew just 0.1% between October and December and 1.5% on an annual basis.
- The Bank said it had acted to curb inflation, but the move was criticised by some analysts.
- The Australian government said the economy remained strong with unemployment at a near 30 year low.
- Australia is raising its benchmark interest rate to its highest level in four years despite signs of a slowdown in the country's economy.
- Stock markets had factored in the likelihood of a rate rise but analysts still expressed concern about the strength of the economy.
