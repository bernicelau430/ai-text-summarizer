- House prices were 11.8% higher on the year in the last quarter of 2004, down from 16.3% in the July-to-September quarter, the Land Registry said.
- The biggest annual gains were made in Wales, where house prices were up by 23% in the fourth quarter.
- The average house price in England and Wales was £182,920, down from £187,971 in July-September.
- House prices rose the slowest in Greater London, being up by 6%.
