- Brazilian government has played down claims that it could step in to save Varig.
- Vice president Jose Alencar says the government is still looking for a solution.
- The airline could win compensation claim against the government.
- Courts awarded Varig 2bn reais ($725m) for freezing tariffs from 1985 to 1992.
- Earlier reports suggested president Luiz Inacio Lula da Silva had decided to intervene.
- Intervention means the government would take administrative control of the company and its finances.
