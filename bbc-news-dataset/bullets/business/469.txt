- Turkey has agreed a draft proposal with the IMF to borrow $10bn, extending financial support until 2007.
- The agreement would enable Turkey to defer payments on previous loans worth $3.7m until 2006.
- Turkey has a huge debt burden, owing $23bn to the IMF.
- Its current account deficit has swelled to $10.7bn this year.
- Turkey has signed a letter of intent stating its determination to push through far-reaching reforms.
- In return, Turkey would be expected to keep inflation under control and introduce market reforms.
