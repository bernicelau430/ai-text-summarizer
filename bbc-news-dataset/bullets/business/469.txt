- Turkey has a large debt burden, owing $23bn to the IMF.
- Its current account deficit has increased to $10.7bn this year.
- Turkey's current $18.6bn loan agreement with the IMF expires in February.
- The new deal would provide added support between 2005 and 2007.
- The agreement would also enable Turkey to defer payments on previous loans worth $3.7m until 2006.
- Turkey has agreed to a draft proposal with the IMF to borrow $10bn, extending its financial support until 2007.
- Turkey signed a letter of intent to push through reforms to its tax, benefits system, and banking sector.
- In return for the funding, Turkey would be expected to control inflation and introduce market reforms.
