- Germany's economy grew at its fastest rate in four years during 2004.
- The growth was driven by strong exports.
- Gross domestic product (GDP) rose by 1.7% last year.
- The economy contracted in 2003.
- Concerns remain over the strength of the euro, weak domestic demand, and a sluggish labor market.
- Analyst: "During 2004 we profited from the fact that the world economy was strong."
- If exports weaken and domestic growth remains poor, we cannot expect much from 2005.
