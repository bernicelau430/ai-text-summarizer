- The European Commission has called a truce in its battle with France and Germany over breaching deficit limits.
- The EU said that 2005 should be the target year for the pair to bring their budgets below 3%.
- The forecasts are based on EC predictions of GDP growth of 1.5% in Germany next year and 2.2% in France.
- France and Germany will breach that this year.
- Under EU rules, member countries must keep their deficits below 3%.
