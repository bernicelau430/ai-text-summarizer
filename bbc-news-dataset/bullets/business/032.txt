- Sumitomo Mitsui Financial has withdrawn its takeover offer for rival bank UFJ Holdings.
- UFJ Holdings can now merge with Mitsubishi Tokyo.
- Sumitomo, Japan's third-largest bank, tabled a higher offer for UFJ, valuing the company at $35bn.
- UFJ's management preferred the offer from Mitsubishi Tokyo Financial Group (MTFG), Japan's second-largest bank.
- Sumitomo cleared the way for UFJ to conclude a 3 trillion yen ($29bn) deal with Mitsubishi.
- Mitsubishi's takeover of UFJ will be Japan's largest-ever takeover deal.
