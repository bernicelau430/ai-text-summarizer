- Deloitte has warned that ten years of "golden" economic performance may come to an end in 2005.
- Deloitte is forecasting economic growth of 2% this year, below Chancellor Gordon Brown's forecast of 3% to 3.5%.
- The UK economy had enjoyed a "golden period" during the past decade with unemployment falling and inflation at its lowest since the 1960s.
- Economic growth is likely to be constrained by increased pressure on household budgets and rising taxes.
- Robust household spending growth is likely to suffer as the housing market slowdown gathers pace.
