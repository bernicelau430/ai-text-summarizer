- Japan's economy grew 2.6% overall last year.
- Forecast to see growth of 2.1% in 2005.
- The Japanese economy has officially gone back into recession for the fourth time in a decade.
- Gross domestic product fell by 0.1% in the last three months of 2004.
- The Tokyo stock market fell after the figures were announced, but rose again on a widespread perception that the economy will recover later this year.
- The economy has some soft patches but if you look at the bigger picture, it is in a recovery stage.
- The fall reflects weak exports and a slowdown in consumer spending, and follows similar falls in GDP in the two previous quarters.
