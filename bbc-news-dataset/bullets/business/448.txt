- US prosecutors have charged nine food suppliers with helping Ahold inflate earnings by more than $800m.
- The suppliers are accused of signing false documents relating to the amount of money they paid the retailer.
- The suppliers are said to have inflated the amount of money they paid, providing auditors with signed letters.
- Ahold is the world's fourth-largest supermarket chain.
- Ahold hit the headlines in February 2003 after accounting irregularities emerged at its US subsidiary Foodservice.
- At the time of the scandal, Ahold was seen by many as Europe's Enron.
- The FBI called the nine vendors the key ingredients in "the process of cooking the books" at Ahold.
