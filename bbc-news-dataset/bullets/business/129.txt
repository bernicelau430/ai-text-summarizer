- 100,000 people are expected to converge on the Brazilian resort of Porto Alegre for the World Social Forum
  - forum for campaigners against globalisation and for fair trade
- PricewaterhouseCoopers survey: four in ten business leaders were 'very confident' that their companies would see sales rise in 2005.
- More than 20 world leaders are expected to attend the Davos forum, including:
  - Mr Blair
  - French President Jacques Chirac
  - South African President Thabo Mbeki
- WHO: 700,000 people in poor countries were on life-extending drugs
  - up from 440,000 six months earlier but amounting to only 12% of the 5.8 million who needed them
- Showbiz figures will also put in an appearance, from U2 frontman Bono to Angelina Jolie.
- Davos forum is dominated by business issues
  - outsourcing to corporate leadership
  - bosses of more than a fifth of the world's 500 largest companies scheduled to attend.
- Climate change and the fight against Aids are leading the list of concerns for the first day of the World Economic Forum.
