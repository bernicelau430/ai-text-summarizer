- UK exports rose by 3.2% to £24.8bn.
- Total imports rose to a record of £27.9bn.
- The difference between exports and imports was £3.1bn.
- The UK's deficit with the EU fell to £1.9bn from £2.1bn.
- Non-EU shortfall dropped to £2.7bn from £2.9bn in October.
- Goods exports were £16.9bn, while imports were £21.5bn.
- Goods deficit was £4.6bn, down from £5bn in October.
- The cumulative deficit for the first eleven months of 2004 stands at £36.3bn.
- EU exports rose 2%, driven by increased sales of chemicals.
