- UK exports rose by 3.2% to £24.8bn.
- Total imports rose to a record of £27.9bn.
- The difference between exports and imports was £3.1bn.
- The UK's deficit with the EU fell to £1.9bn from £2.1bn.
- Non-EU shortfall dropped to £2.7bn from £2.9bn in October.
- The UK exported £16.9bn worth of goods, but imported £21.5bn.
- The deficit for goods alone was £4.6bn, down from October's £5bn.
- The cumulative deficit for the first eleven months of 2004 now stands at £36.3bn.
- EU exports rose 2%, fuelled by an increase in sales of chemicals.
