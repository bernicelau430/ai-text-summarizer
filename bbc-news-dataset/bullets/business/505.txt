- China accounted for 20.1% of Japan's trade in 2004.
- The US accounted for 18.6% of Japan's trade in 2004.
- During 2004, Japan's trade surplus grew 17.9% to 12.011 trillion yen.
- More than half the surplus, 6.962 trillion yen, was accounted for by its trade with the US.
- China overtook the US to become Japan's biggest trading partner in 2004.
- This is the highest figure for Japanese trade with China since records began in 1947.
