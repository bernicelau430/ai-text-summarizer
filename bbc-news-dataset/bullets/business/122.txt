- The SEC reached a $140m deal in its probe into Columbia Management Advisors (CAM) and Columbia Funds Distributor (CFD).
- The SEC said it had brought fraud charges against two ex-senior executives of Columbia Distributor.
- Three other ex-Columbia executives agreed settlements with the SEC.
- The SEC agreed a deal totalling $375m with Banc of America Capital Management, BACAP Distributors and Banc of America Securities.
- In both cases, it said there had been secret deals to engage in market timing in mutual fund shares.
