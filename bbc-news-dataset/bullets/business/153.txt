- House prices have risen 0.9% this year, according to Nationwide.
- Prices went up by 0.4% in January on the month and by 12.6% on a year earlier.
- New mortgage loans in January fell to 79,000.
- Mortgage approvals fell to a near 10-year low.
- The annual rate has halved since August last year.
- Homeowners expect house prices to rise by 1% over the next six months.
- 48,000 fewer mortgages were approved in January than in 2004.
