- Deloitte warns that ten years of economic performance may end in 2005 with growth slowing.
- Deloitte forecasts 2% economic growth this year, below Chancellor Gordon Brown's forecast of 3% to 3.5%.
- The UK economy had a golden period with unemployment at a near 30-year low and inflation at its lowest since the 1960s.
- Growth is likely to be constrained by increased pressure on household budgets and rising taxes.
- Household spending growth, a main driver of the economy, is likely to suffer as the housing market slows down.
- This will result in a marked slowdown in growth in 2005 and 2006 compared to last year's 3.25% expansion.
