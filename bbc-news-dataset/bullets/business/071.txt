- Asahi is predicting profits to rise 50% in 2005 as it launches a drink based on soybean peptides rather than malt.
- Japanese brewers are increasingly making money from beer-flavored drinks rather than beer itself
- Market for so-called beer-like drinks is set to grow 84% this year.
- Beer and spirits are heavily taxed in Japan, driving breweries to search for alternatives.
- Despite a record profit in 2004 of 30.6bn yen, Asahi is coming late to the market.
