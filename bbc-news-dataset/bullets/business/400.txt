- Monsanto has agreed to pay a $1.5m fine for bribing an Indonesian official.
- A former senior manager at Monsanto directed a consulting firm to give a $50,000 bribe in 2002.
- The bribe was intended to avoid environmental impact studies on its cotton.
- Monsanto faced both criminal and civil charges from the Department of Justice and the SEC.
- Monsanto will pay $1m to the Department of Justice and adopt internal compliance measures.
- Monsanto admitted to paying bribes to high-ranking officials between 1997 and 2002.
