- China overtook the US to become Japan's biggest trading partner in 2004.
- China accounted for 20.1% of Japan's trade in 2004, compared with 18.6% for the US.
- This is the highest figure for Japanese trade with China since records began in 1947.
- During 2004, Japan's trade surplus grew 17.9% to 12.011 trillion yen, with more than half the surplus accounted for by trade with the US.
