- US car firms General Motors (GM) and Ford have been forced to cut production in the face of falling car sales.
- US sales at GM sank 12.7% in February compared to a year ago, while Ford sales dropped 3%.
- Chrysler was the only Detroit based automaker to boast an increase in market share.
- Meanwhile, Ford said it was cutting first-quarter North American production by another 10,000 vehicles.
- Japan's top car maker Toyota and Nissan saw sales accelerate.
