- More than 90% of large companies around the world are highly optimistic about their economic prospects.
- The survey included 1,300 bosses.
- When PwC conducted the same survey two years ago, nearly 30% of bosses were gloomy about their prospects.
- The worry about low-cost competition has slightly fallen from last year, with just 54% of companies calling it a "significant threat" or "one of the biggest threats".
- According to Frank Brown, the trend of large companies to have global operations has one clear upside.
  - One risk in one region won't kill your business anymore.
- PwC interviewed 1,324 chief executives throughout the world during the last three months of 2004.
- Samuel DiPiazza said a growing number of companies were also concerned that moves to outsource work to cheaper countries could hurt their reputation in their home markets and harm the quality of service they provide to their customers.
