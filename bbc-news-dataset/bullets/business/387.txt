- The German economy's three years of stagnation ended in 2004.
- German unemployment rose for the 11th consecutive month in December.
- The year's average jobless total was the highest since reunification.
- The economy has been too weak to create work.
- The Hartz IV reforms should help cut the average jobless rate to between 3% and 5% by 2010.
- A strong and lasting turnaround is not expected until 2006.
