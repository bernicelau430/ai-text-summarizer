- The founder and former boss of Parmalat has apologized to investors who lost money due to the firm's collapse.
- More than 130,000 people lost money following the firm's collapse.
- Mr. Tanzi spent several months in jail and was kept under house arrest until last September.
- He alleged that certain companies were aware of Parmalat's true financial state but continued lending money.
- Parmalat is now run by a state-appointed administrator, Enrico Bondi, who has launched lawsuits against 80 banks to recover money.
- Two former Parmalat auditors will stand trial for their role in the firm's collapse.
