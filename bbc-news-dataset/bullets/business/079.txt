- The Federal Trade Commission said two in five of the 635,173 reports it had from consumers concerned ID fraud.
- The biggest slice of the 246,570 ID fraud cases reported - almost 30% - concerned abuses of people's credit.
- Internet auctions were the second biggest source of fraud complaints, comprising 16% of the total.
- Catalogue and home-shopping frauds were next in line, accounting for 8% of total complaints.
- Concerns about internet services and computers amounted to 6% of complaints.
- The total cost of fraud reported by consumers was $546m.
