- Gazprom and Rosneft merger is going ahead without Yugansk.
- Yugansk will be spun off as a separate, state-owned oil firm.
- Sergei Bogdanchikov, current Rosneft chief, will head Yugansk.
- Gazprom chief Alexei Miller states Gazprom needs Yugansk ultimately.
- The merger will allow foreigners to trade in Gazprom shares.
- The state wanted to control Gazprom before allowing foreigners to trade.
- The deal will give Gazprom control of 8% of Russia's total oil production.
- Yugansk was sold to a shell company in a disputed auction in December.
