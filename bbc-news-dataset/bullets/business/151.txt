- The trial of Bernie Ebbers, former chief executive of bankrupt WorldCom, has started in New York with jury selection.
- Mr. Ebbers, 63, is accused of masterminding an $11bn accounting fraud that led to the firm's collapse in July 2002.
- Under Mr. Ebbers' leadership, WorldCom became a $160bn telecoms giant.
- The trial, expected to last two months, is the latest attempt by US prosecutors to pursue senior executives for fraud.
- If found guilty, Mr. Ebbers could face a substantial jail sentence.
