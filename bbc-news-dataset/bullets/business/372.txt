- Madagascar has completed the replacement of its Malagasy franc with the ariary.
- The ariary was a pre-colonial currency.
- The phasing out of the franc started in July 2003.
- The reason for the change was to distance the country from French colonial rule.
- The change also addresses the issue of counterfeit francs.
- All prices and contracts must be quoted in ariary.
- The ariary is trading at 1,893 to the US dollar.
