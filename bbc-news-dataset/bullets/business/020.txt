- The number of calls to call centres is growing at a rate of 20% every year.
- The average induction time for a call centre worker fell last year from 36 to just 21 days.
- There are "no big differences in wait time and call resolution" between call centres based in Europe or North America and those in developing countries around the world.
- Customers trying to get through to call centres are getting impatient and quicker to hang up.
- Call centres also saw a sharp increase of customers simply abandoning calls, from just over 5% in 2003 to a record 13.3% during last year.
- Call centres have a high "churn rate", with nearly a quarter of workers throwing in the towel every year.
