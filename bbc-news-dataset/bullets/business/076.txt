- The Palestinian economy is performing well below its potential, said a World Bank report.
- There is a close link between the number of closures and Palestinian economic problems.
- The Palestinian economy lost more than 22,000 jobs during the first half of this year.
- 104,000 new jobs were created last year during a brief easing of violence and closures.
- Young people are particularly hard hit with 37% out of work, compared with 14% four years ago.
- Even more would be on the poverty line without an average of $950m a year from international donors, some of which goes towards job creation.
