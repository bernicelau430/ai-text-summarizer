- The European Commission has launched an investigation into Alitalia regarding illegal state aid.
- The Italian government and Alitalia deny that the money is state aid, claiming it's part of a restructuring plan.
- Alitalia received state aid in 1997.
- Rival carriers claim Alitalia has violated state aid rules and threatened competition.
- The Commission aims to verify that the restructuring plan contains no illegal state aid.
- EU regulations allow government financial help to companies only on the same terms as a commercial investor.
- Eight of Alitalia's rivals, including Lufthansa, British Airways, and Iberia, brought the matter to the Commission's attention.
