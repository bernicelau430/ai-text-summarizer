- The UK's jobless total rose for the second month in a row in December
- The number of people out of work rose 32,000 to 1.41 million in the last three months of 2004, even as 90,000 more people were in employment
- Throughout 2004, the number of people in work increased by 296,000 to 28.52 million - the highest figure since records began in 1971
- The manufacturing sector continued to suffer, with 104,000 workers axed during the last quarter of 2004
