- A slump in profitability at luxury car maker Mercedes has prompted a big drop in profits at parent DaimlerChrysler.
- The German-US carmaker saw fourth quarter operating profits fall to 785m euros ($1bn) from 2.4bn euros in 2003.
- For the year as a whole, its operating profits fell 46% to 1.6bn euros.
- The US division - whose marques include Dodge and Jeep - transformed a full year operating loss of 506m euros in 2003 into a 1.4bn euros profit last year.
