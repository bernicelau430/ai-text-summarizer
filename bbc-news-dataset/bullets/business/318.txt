- Deloitte is forecasting economic growth of 2% this year, below Chancellor Gordon Brown's forecast of 3% to 3.5%.
- The UK economy had enjoyed a "golden period" during the past decade.
- Economic growth is likely to be constrained during the next few years by increased pressure on household budgets and rising taxes.
- "Whereas the main driver of the economy in recent years has been robust household spending growth, this is likely to suffer as the housing market slowdown gathers pace."
- "This will result in a marked slowdown in growth in 2005 and 2006 compared to last year, when the economy expanded by 3.25%."
