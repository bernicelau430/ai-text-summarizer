- President Bush presented his 2006 budget, cutting domestic spending to lower the deficit.
- Military spending is set to rise by 4.8%, to reach $419.3bn.
- The budget does not include the cost of military operations in Iraq and Afghanistan.
- The state department's planned budget would rise to just under $23bn.
- Mr. Bush has promised to halve the US's massive budget deficit within five years.
- The deficit, partly the result of massive tax cuts, has been a key factor in pushing the US dollar lower.
