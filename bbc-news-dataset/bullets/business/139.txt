- The trial of Bernie Ebbers, former chief executive of bankrupt US phone company WorldCom, has started in New York.
- Mr. Ebbers, 63, is accused of being the mastermind behind an $11bn accounting fraud that eventually saw the firm collapse in July 2002.
- Under Mr. Ebbers' leadership, WorldCom emerged from Mississippi obscurity to become a $160bn telecoms giant.
- Mr. Ebbers' trial, which is expected to last two months, is the latest in a series of attempts by US prosecutors to pursue senior executives for fraud.
- If found guilty, Mr. Ebbers could face a substantial jail sentence.
