- Kraft plans to cut back on advertising of products like Oreo cookies and sugary Kool-Aid drinks.
- This is part of an effort to promote healthy eating.
- Commercials for some snacks and cereals will be replaced by food and drink qualifying for Kraft's new "Sensible Solution" label.
- Kraft will also add a label to its more nutritional and low-fat brands to promote the benefits.
- The new advertising policy covers TV, radio, and print publications, and is aimed at children between the ages of six and 11.
