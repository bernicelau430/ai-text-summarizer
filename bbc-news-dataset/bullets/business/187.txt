- A US bank will donate more than $8m to victims of Augusto Pinochet's regime.
- The bank had been accused of illegally concealing Gen Pinochet's assets.
- Riggs Bank pleaded guilty to failing to report suspicious activity relating to accounts held by Gen Pinochet.
- The bank agreed to create a fund for the victims, but the charges were dropped.
- Riggs Bank will put money in a special fund managed by the Salvador Allende Foundation.
