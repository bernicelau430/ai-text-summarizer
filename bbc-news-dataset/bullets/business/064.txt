- Lufthansa may sue federal agencies for damages after the arrival of US president George W Bush disrupted flights.
- Lufthansa said that it may lose millions of euros as a result of Air Force One landing at Frankfurt airport.
- 92 outgoing flights and 86 income flights were delayed by an average of an hour following President Bush's arrival, affecting almost 17,000 passengers.
- Lufthansa did not plan to pursue Germany's air traffic controllers' organisation or the airport authority.
- Lufthansa wanted instead to see if it was possible to sue the German federal agencies that gave the orders.
