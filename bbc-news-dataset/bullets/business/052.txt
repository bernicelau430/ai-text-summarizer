- Italian Prime Minister Silvio Berlusconi will unveil plans aimed at kickstarting the country's sputtering economy.
- The government will focus on opening up markets, infrastructure, research, making more incentives available, bankruptcy law, the slow pace of the justice system.
- Mr Berlusconi has previously promised to cut taxes by 6.5bn euros this year.
- Mr Berlusconi is expected to table reforms aimed at boosting research and development (R&D) spending, and the competitiveness of small firms.
- Business and consumer confidence has dipped and analysts have raised concerns that what little spending there is stems from Italians dipping into their savings accounts or using credit cards.
