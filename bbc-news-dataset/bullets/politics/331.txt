- Welsh councils should set their taxes at "reasonable levels" after being given an average funding increase of 6%, says the assembly government.
- Finance Minister Sue Essex said it was a "good deal" for local government.
- She said council taxes could be kept at reasonable levels, "even for those people who have moved up a band".
- Welsh Liberal Democrat council leaders are doing their utmost to keep council tax to a minimum.
- Conservative local government spokesman Glyn Davies said that the minister was taking spin to the very verge of deception.
