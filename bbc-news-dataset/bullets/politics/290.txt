- Cutting the drink-drive limit from 80mg to 50mg would save 65 lives a year, according to research.
- It would also lead to 230 fewer serious injuries compared with 2003.
- The AA Motoring Trust is unsure of the effect on drivers' attitudes to drink-driving.
- The bill includes changes to speeding fines, not the drink-drive limit.
- Ministers don't support a lower limit, saying it's unclear if it would have a major impact.
- Road safety campaigners say staggered speeding fines will reduce incentive to stay within limits in residential areas.
