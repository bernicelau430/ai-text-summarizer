- People staying in Britain for six months or more from countries with high TB rates would need a chest x-ray and further tests.
- People staying less than six months would only be tested if working in health care, childcare, or teaching.
- Applicants must prove they have an acceptable standard of health and are unlikely to be a danger to public health or impose significant costs on the NHS.
- Home Office Minister Des Browne says TB checks are already routine for those from high-risk countries staying six months or more.
- The Tories say people fleeing persecution will not be denied sanctuary due to poor health.
- Those diagnosed with TB would need treatment at home before entering the UK.
- Government figures show TB in England has increased by 25% in the last 10 years, with nearly two-thirds of cases born overseas.
