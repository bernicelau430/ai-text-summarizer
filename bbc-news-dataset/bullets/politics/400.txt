- Former Labour prime minister Lord Callaghan has died on the eve of his 93rd birthday.
- He held each of the major offices of chancellor, home secretary, foreign secretary and prime minister during his career.
- Conservative peer Lord Heseltine said that despite their political differences, he and Lord Callaghan became friends.
- Lord Callaghan, who leaves a son and two daughters, was the longest living former British PM in history.
- In the autumn of 1978, before the Winter of Discontent, Lord Callaghan refused to hold an early election which may have delivered a Labour victory.
