- University College London research claims cutting the drink-drive limit would save 65 lives a year.
- It would also lead to 230 fewer serious injuries.
- Road safety head Andrew Howard questioned whether social disapproval of drinking drivers would stay at the current rate.
- The AA Motoring Trust was unsure of the effect on drivers' attitudes.
- The bill includes changes to speeding fines, but not to the drink-drive limit.
- Ministers do not support a lower limit, as they're unsure of its effect.
- Plans are in place for staggered speeding fines, more heavily penalizing drivers who exceed limits by a wide margin.
