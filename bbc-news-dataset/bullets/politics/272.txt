- The environment select committee said sloppy wording of the draft animal welfare bill could leave anglers facing court even though it was not intended.
- The committee also said some legal protections for animals were downgraded by the bill.
- The MPs said they were "concerned" the government had not consulted directly on its plans to improve animal welfare.
- The bill would allow animal welfare officers to intervene to prevent harm to an animal.
- The bill would outlaw "mutilation" of animals.
- The draft bill would allow certain acts that should be prosecuted to go unpunished.
- Any change in the law as it affects animals always arouses powerful emotions and great public interest.
