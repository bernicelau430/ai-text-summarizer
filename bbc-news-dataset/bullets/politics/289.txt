- Neil Kinnock has officially been made a life peer in the House of Lords.
- He was accompanied by Lords Leader Baroness Amos and Baroness Royall of Blaisdon.
- Kinnock led Labour from 1983 until 1992 and was recently an EU commissioner.
- He will advocate for reform of the House of Lords and discuss issues like higher education.
- He will be known as Baron Kinnock of Bedwellty, after his former constituency.
- Kinnock has also become chairman of the British Council.
- He accepted the peerage to be a working peer for political reasons.
