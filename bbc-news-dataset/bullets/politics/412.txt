- The Environment Select Committee has raised concerns about the wording of the draft animal welfare bill.
- Some legal protections for animals were downgraded by the bill.
- The MPs said they were concerned the government had not consulted directly on its plans to improve animal welfare.
- The draft bill seeks to modernize and improve animal welfare legislation.
- It intends to protect kept animals and companion animals by setting up a duty of care.
- Committee chair said any change in the law as it affects animals always arouses powerful emotions and great public interest.
