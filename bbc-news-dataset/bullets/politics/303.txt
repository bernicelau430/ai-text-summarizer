- Sir Edward Heath resigned and the Queen invited Mr. Wilson to form a new minority government.
- Heath told Mr. Thorpe that his colleagues would not agree to serve under any other prime minister.
- Thorpe told Heath he was sorry for the difficult situation.
- Heath considered resigning and returning to coalition government in a Labour-led coalition.
- Heath and Thorpe initially thought they had the makings of a deal for a Conservative-Liberal pact.
- Thorpe's colleagues refused to support him.
- Wilson would not form a coalition with either the Liberals or Conservatives due to opposition from the Labour left.
