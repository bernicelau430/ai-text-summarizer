- The draft animal welfare bill could leave anglers facing court, the environment select committee said.
- The committee said some legal protections for animals were downgraded by the bill.
- The MPs were "concerned" the government had not consulted directly on its plans.
- The bill would allow animal welfare officers to intervene to prevent harm to an animal.
- It would outlaw "mutilation" of animals unless it's in the animal's best interest.
- Sloppy wording in the bill would allow certain acts to go unpunished.
- Committee chairman Michael Jack said changes in animal law always arouse powerful emotions.
