- Conservative Party would cut the number of MPs by about one-fifth if elected.
- Changes could be implemented by the election after next.
- A referendum would be held in Wales to decide whether or not to scrap the Welsh Assembly.
- Average size of an English constituency was 70,000 people.
- The plan would also even out the "great unfairness" of there being proportionately more Scottish and Welsh MPs.
- The party would also reduce the number of government special advisers.
