- The Tories have promised a reduction on bills for pensioners.
- Labour says it wants to retain the property-based tax but reform it to make it fairer.
- The Liberal Democrats want to do away with the council tax altogether.
- Instead households where council tax payers are over 65 would get a rebate covering half their bill, up to a maximum of £500.
- The party says it would aim for a £5,000 tax-free personal allowance, or a £7,000 allowance for the over-65s.
