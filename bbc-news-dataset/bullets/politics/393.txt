- Archives hold personal details of people far removed from politics and Parliament.
- Until 1857, the only way in England to get a full divorce which allowed re-marriage was to obtain an Act of Parliament.
- The archives hold records of high politics for both Houses of Parliament, including copies of all acts passed since 1497.
- The oldest record deals with the employment of workers in the woollen industry in Norfolk.
- Among the records is the story of Jane Campbell, the first woman ever to divorce her husband.
- There are details of foreign nationals made British citizens by act of Parliament, including composer George Frideric Handel in 1727.
- Archives are open to the public at the House of Lords Record Office, with visitors able to phone ahead when they want to view particular items.
