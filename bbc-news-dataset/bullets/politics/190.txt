- Michael Howard admitted to playing truant to play snooker in Llanelli in the 1950s.
- He said his time at Jack's Snooker Hall did not do him "any lasting damage."
- Howard eventually left Llanelli Grammar School and the snooker hall to go to Cambridge University.
- Fellow Welshman Ray Reardon, known as "Dracula," won the snooker world championship six times.
- Reardon left school at 14 to work as a miner.
- Terry Griffiths, also from Llanelli, won the tournament in 1979.
- Tony Meo and another player spent all of their spare time at the snooker hall.
