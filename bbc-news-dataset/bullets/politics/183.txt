- Former Northern Ireland minister Sir Nicholas Scott has died.
- He dealt with education and security as a Northern Ireland minister.
- He rode out the mass IRA escape from the Maze in 1983.
- After leaving Northern Ireland, he became a UK disability minister.
- Sir Nicholas was replaced as the member for Kensington and Chelsea by Alan Clark.
- He was deselected by his constituency in December 1996.
- Sir Nicholas claimed painkillers reacted badly with alcohol.
- He was a strong defender of the Anglo-Irish Agreement.
- He was held in esteem in Dublin government and SDLP circles.
- His daughter, Victoria, denounced his actions and called for his resignation.
