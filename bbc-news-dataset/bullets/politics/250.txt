- A committee of MPs warned that anglers and fishermen could find themselves prosecuted under the draft animal welfare bill.
- Some legal protections for animals were downgraded by the bill.
- The MPs were "concerned" the government had not consulted directly on its plans to improve animal welfare.
- The draft bill seeks to modernize and improve animal welfare legislation and intends to protect "kept animals" and "companion" animals by setting up a "duty of care".
- The bill would allow animal welfare officers to intervene to prevent harm to an animal and outlaw "mutilation" of animals unless it can be demonstrated to be in the animal's best interest.
- The draft bill would allow certain acts that should be prosecuted to go unpunished.
- Committee chairman Michael Jack said that any change in the law as it affects animals always arouses powerful emotions and great public interest.
