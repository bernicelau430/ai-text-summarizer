- The Tories have promised a reduction on bills for pensioners.
- Labour wants to retain the property-based tax but reform it.
- They say the council tax is the most unpopular and most unfair tax.
- It would not be means tested.
- Instead households where council tax payers are over 65 would get a rebate covering half their bill, up to a maximum of £500.
- The party would aim for a £5,000 tax-free personal allowance, or a £7,000 allowance for the over-65s.
- The Liberal Democrats want to switch to a local income tax.
