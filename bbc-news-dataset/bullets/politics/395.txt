- Cutting the drink-drive limit from 80mg to 50mg would save 65 lives a year.
- It would also lead to 230 fewer serious injuries on the UK's roads.
- AA Motoring Trust said it was not sure what affect dropping the limit would have on drivers' attitudes to drink-driving.
- The bill includes changes to speeding fines but not to the drink-drive limit.
- Ministers do not support a lower limit.
