- Research claims cutting the drink-drive limit from 80mg to 50mg would save 65 lives a year.
- Cutting the limit would also lead to 230 fewer serious injuries.
- The bill includes changes to speeding fines but not to the drink-drive limit.
- Road safety campaigners say the bill will reduce the incentive for drivers to stay within the limits.
- The AA Motoring Trust is unsure what effect dropping the limit would have.
- Ministers do not support a lower limit, saying it's unclear it would have a major impact.
