- Welsh councils should set their taxes at "reasonable levels" after being given an average funding increase of 6%, says the assembly government.
- Finance Minister Sue Essex said it was a "good deal" for local government.
- Opposition parties said rebanding of council taxes would mean steep rises.
- Conservative local government spokesman Glyn Davies said that for the minister to claim that the majority of council tax payers in Wales should see very little change "is taking spin to the very verge of deception".
