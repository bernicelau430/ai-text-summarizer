- Lord Scarman, who conducted the inquiry into the 1981 Brixton riots, has died aged 93.
- Lord Falconer said Lord Scarman was one of the 'great advocates of our generation'.
- Lord Woolf said Lord Scarman's 'pioneering work' paved the way for the Human Rights Act 1998.
- Lord Scarman will be remembered for the public inquiry he led into a string of race riots which began in Brixton.
- Elizabeth Butler-Sloss said Lord Scarman was a 'good and humane judge'.
- Trevor Phillips praised Lord Scarman's 'ability to listen'.
