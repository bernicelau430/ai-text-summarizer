- A new book about Gordon Brown claims that Tony Blair promised Gordon Brown he would stand down before the next election.
- The book, Brown's Britain, said Tony Blair felt by November 2003 he had lost voters' trust.
- The author's sources, all unnamed 
allies

 of Mr Blair and Mr Brown, said the prime minister felt the Iraq war had undermined him and that he was no longer an asset to the Labour Party.
- But the prime minister changed his mind following intervention from allies in the Cabinet.
- The book alleges that Mr Blair told the chancellor at a dinner hosted by deputy PM John Prescott in November 2003 of his intention to stand down.
- The book by Sunday Telegraph journalist Robert Peston said the pair had 
mutual animosity and contempt

 for each other.
