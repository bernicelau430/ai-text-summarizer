- The Tories promise a reduction on bills for pensioners, who they say have been hardest hit by year-on-year increases in council tax.
- Labour wants to retain the property-based tax but reform it to make it fairer and says there is scope for councils to become more efficient.
- They say the council tax is the most unpopular and most unfair tax in Britain.
- It would not be means tested, say the Tories.
- They are already helping pensioners with council tax bills.
- The rate would be set locally, but administered by the Inland Revenue which they say would save at least £300m.
- Households where council tax payers are over 65 would get a rebate covering half their bill, up to a maximum of £500.
- The party would aim for a £5,000 tax-free personal allowance.
- The Liberal Democrats want to switch to a local income tax.
