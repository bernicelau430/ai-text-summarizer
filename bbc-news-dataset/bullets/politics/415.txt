- Sir Edward told the nation he was resigning and the Queen invited Mr Wilson to form a new minority government.
- The PM told Mr Thorpe that his colleagues would not agree to serve under any other prime minister.
- Mr Thorpe told Sir Edward: "I am sorry this is obviously hell - a nightmare on stilts for you."
- Sir Edward mulled over resigning and perhaps returning to coalition government in a Labour-led coalition.
- Hours later Sir Edward's hopes of a deal unravelled as Mr Thorpe's colleagues refused to support him.
- Mr Wilson would not form a coalition with either the Liberals or the Conservatives because of the opposition of the Labour left.
- Both Sir Edward and Mr Thorpe thought they had the makings of a deal.
