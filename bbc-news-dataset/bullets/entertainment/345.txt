- U2 wants to be the biggest band ever
- The band has done "everything in their considerable powers" to ensure they remain the biggest band in the world, according to Q magazine editor Paul Rees
- Bono: "The challenge is to be bigger and bolder and better - to make records the whole world will listen to"
- U2 has won three Grammy Awards for Vertigo
- The end of the decade marked a crucial point for the band - they had reached the top but still yearned for new challenges and achievements
- In 1987, The Joshua Tree broke sales records and saw the band reach the height of their powers with hits including Where the Streets Have No Name, I Still Haven't Found What I'm Looking For and With Or Without You
- Their third album, War, saw them make the breakthrough on both sides of the Atlantic, going to number one in the UK and 12 in the US
- Their new album, How To Dismantle An Atomic Bomb, comes 28 years after the schoolfriends got together in Dublin and 17 years after The Joshua Tree cemented their place on the all-time rock A-list
- Vertigo, the first single from the new album, went straight into the UK singles chart at number one
- Before the release of How To Dismantle An Atomic Bomb, they had sold 125 million albums around the world
