- The release of a film about the Mumbai blasts in 1993 has been postponed following protests.
- 36 people accused in connection with the blasts said it should not be screened until the trial is over.
- Director Anurag Kashyap said the film's release should not be delayed as the book has already been on sale for two years.
- The film Black Friday had been due to open across India.
- This is the second time that those accused in connection with the blasts have sought legal intervention regarding the film.
