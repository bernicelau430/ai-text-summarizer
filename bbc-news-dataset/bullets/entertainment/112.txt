- British films produced in the UK fell 40% in 2004
- UK Film Council said drop was partly due to 2003 being an especially good year
- Government closed tax loopholes film investors used to fund films in the UK
- Strength of the pound against the dollar is expected to have resulted in a fall in the number of US films shot in the UK
- It is "too early" to predict whether the downward trend in UK production would continue throughout 2005
- UK film tax relief scheme Section 48 to announce a replacement in July
