- The first Academy Awards were handed out in 1929 at the Hollywood Roosevelt Hotel.
- The Academy was set up in 1927 as a non-profit organisation with 36 members.
- Douglas Fairbanks Sr was the first president.
- The first film to win was Wings, which starred Clara Bow.
- For the first 10 years or so, the winners list was handed to the newspapers.
- In 1940 the winners were published in the Los Angeles Times at 8.45pm.
- Writer Dudley Nichols refused his Academy Award in 1935 because the Writers' Guild was striking.
- In support of the war effort, the Academy handed out plaster Oscar statuettes during WWII.
- The first televised ceremony was in 1953, with Bob Hope as the MC.
- Three people have refused Oscars, including actor George C Scott.
- Some of the criteria for admittance includes: film credits that reflect the high standards of the Academy, receipt of an Academy Award nomination, achievement of unique distinction, earning of special merit, or making an outstanding contribution to film.
