- The American Film Institute (AFI) hailed Mel Gibson's The Passion of the Christ and Michael Moore's Fahrenheit 9/11 as inspiring national debate.
- The AFI cited Marlon Brando's death and the changing landscape of TV news as key influences.
- The AFI fears the loss of balance and integrity in news, with "newscasters more personalities than journalists."
- The AFI questioned "the long-term viability of evening news broadcasts" due to 24-hour news channels and the internet.
- The AFI jury stated that Marlon Brando's death divided screen acting into "Before Brando" and "After Brando."
