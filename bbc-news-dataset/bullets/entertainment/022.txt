- The RAD launched a competition to reverse the decline in British ballet participation.
- Research shows fewer children over 10 are attending ballet classes and exams.
- Exam participation drops almost 70% after age 10 or 11.
- Only two out of 16 principal dancers at the Royal Ballet are British.
- The competition is aimed at children aged 10 to 13.
- Young ballet dancers are unwilling to make the sacrifices to succeed.
- The RAD aims to reignite passion and nurture young dancers for the future of ballet.
- A spokesman noted only two out of 12 principal dancers at the English National Ballet were British
