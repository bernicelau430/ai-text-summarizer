- The number of British films produced in the UK fell 40% in 2004.
- Johnny Depp and Jude Law movies were among those affected.
- The UK Film Council said 2003 was an especially good year for British film production.
- The government closed tax loopholes that many film investors had taken advantage of.
- The strength of the pound against the dollar is expected to have resulted in a fall in the number of US films shot in the UK.
- The UK Film Council is due to announce a replacement for the UK film tax relief scheme Section 48 in July.
