- Alicia Markova was the UK's first prima ballerina of the modern age.
- She was the greatest in the western world during her prime.
- Markova's birth name was Lilian Alicia Marks, born in London in 1910.
- She became director of ballet for the Metropolitan Opera Ballet.
- She was a full-time Professor of Ballet and Performing Arts at the University of Cincinnati.
- She helped launch the Ballet Club, Vic Wells Ballet, and the Markova-Dolin Ballet.
- Diaghilev changed her name without consulting her.
- She joined Diaghilev in Monte Carlo at 14 after recovering from diphtheria.
- She was one of the first British ballerinas in Les Sylphides.
- She was also outstanding in The Dying Swan.
