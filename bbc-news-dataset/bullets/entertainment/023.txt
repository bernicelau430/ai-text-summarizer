- Marcello Viotti, director of La Fenice Theatre, died in Germany at 50.
- Viotti was director of La Fenice since 2002.
- He conducted at renowned opera houses, including La Scala and the Vienna State Opera.
- His time at La Fenice coincided with its reopening in 2003 after a 1996 fire.
- He directed Massenet's Le Roi de Lahore, Thais, Verdi's La Traviata, and Strauss's Ariadne auf Naxos at La Fenice.
- His Metropolitan Opera debut was in 2000 with Puccini's Madame Butterfly.
