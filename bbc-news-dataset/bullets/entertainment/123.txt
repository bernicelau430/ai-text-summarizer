- British musicians are "disadvantaged" compared to US musicians, says Nigel McCune of the Musicians' Union.
- Music Managers' Forum (MMF) says British artists face "an uphill struggle" to succeed in the US.
- The US is the world's biggest music market.
- British acts are prevented from maintaining momentum and developing in the US due to creaky bureaucracy, says McCune.
- A singer hoping to perform in the US can expect to pay $1,300 simply for obtaining a visa.
