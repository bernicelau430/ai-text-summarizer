- Ossie Davis was found dead in his hotel room in Miami Beach, Florida.
- He was making a film at the time of his death.
- Davis was a prominent figure in the civil rights movement and was a voice for racial equality.
- He had a 65-year career as a producer, director, actor and writer for stage and screen.
- His film debut, in 1950, was in the film No Way Out, starring Sydney Poitier and Ruby Dee.
- Miami Beach police spokesman said the cause of death appeared to be natural.
- Davis and Dee were married for more than 56 years and together received Kennedy Center honors in 2004.
