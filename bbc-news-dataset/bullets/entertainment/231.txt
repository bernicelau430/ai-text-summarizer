- The number of British films produced in the UK fell 40% in 2004.
- UK Film Council: The drop was partly due to 2003 being an especially good year.
- The strength of the pound against the dollar is expected to have resulted in a fall in the number of US films shot in the UK.
- It was "too early" to predict whether the downward trend would continue throughout 2005.
- Government closed tax loopholes that many film investors had taken advantage of.
- UK Film Council is also due to announce a replacement for UK film tax relief scheme Section 48 in July.
- "The reduction in indigenous film production in 2004 was due to a variety of factors, including the continuing long-term trend towards co-production of films in more than one country and changes to financing arrangements."
