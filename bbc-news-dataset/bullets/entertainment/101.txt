- The Sundance Film Festival will feature two new international cinema competitions during its January 20-30 season in Utah.
- International films will be given the same prominence as US films, with a focus on movies dominated by the theme of war.
- Twelve films competing in the world cinema documentary category focus on countries and people under siege.
- The 16 films in the world cinema dramatic category include works from Germany, South Korea, Angola, China, Denmark, and Australia.
- Forty-two films will debut at Sundance.
- The Liberace of Baghdad, by British director Sean McAllister, features an Iraqi pianist hiding in a hotel awaiting a visa.
- The Three Rooms of Melancholia, a Finnish film, looks at the war in Chechnya.
