- Arthur Hailey, known for bestselling novels, died at 84.
- He meticulously researched professions for his novels, drawing characters from real life.
- Hailey was inspired to write his first screenplay, Flight into Danger, on a flight to Toronto.
- Flight into Danger was adapted into his first novel, Runaway Zero-Eight.
- His greatest ambition was to see his name on a book.
- Hotel (1965) brought Hollywood's attention.
- Airport was adapted into a hit film in 1970.
- Strong Medicine was turned into a film in 1986.
- Despite lukewarm reviews, Hailey remained popular in the seventies.
