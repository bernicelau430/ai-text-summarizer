- UK film production fell 40% in 2004.
- Twenty-seven British films were made in the UK last year, compared with 45 in 2003.
- The drop was partly due to 2003 being a good year for British film production.
- The government closed tax loopholes that many film investors had taken advantage of.
- The strength of the pound against the dollar is expected to have resulted in a fall in the number of US films shot in the UK.
- It is also due to announce a replacement for UK film tax relief scheme Section 48 in July.
- The reduction in indigenous film production in 2004 was due to various factors, including co-production of films and changes to financing arrangements.
