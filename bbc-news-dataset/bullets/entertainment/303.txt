- The number of British films produced in the UK fell 40% in 2004, compared to 2003.
- 27 British films were made in the UK last year, compared to 45 in 2003.
- Movies starring Johnny Depp and Jude Law were among those affected.
- The UK Film Council cited 2003 as an exceptionally good year for British film production.
- The UK Film Council said it was too early to predict if the downward trend would continue throughout 2005.
- The government closed tax loopholes used by film investors.
- The UK Film Council is due to announce a replacement for Section 48 in July.
- The pound's strength against the dollar is expected to reduce US films shot in the UK.
- Co-production of films in multiple countries and changes to financing arrangements also contributed to the reduction.
