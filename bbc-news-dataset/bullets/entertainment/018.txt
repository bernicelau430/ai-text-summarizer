- The American Film Institute (AFI) cited Marlon Brando's death and the changing landscape of TV news as key influences and trends in film and broadcasting.
- The AFI jury stated that screen acting is divided into "Before Brando" and "After Brando."
- The AFI is concerned about the viability of evening news broadcasts because of 24-hour news channels and the internet.
- The AFI fears news is becoming more about personalities than journalists and that balance and integrity are being ignored.
- The AFI hailed Mel Gibson's biblical epic and Michael Moore's political documentary for inspiring national debate.
