- The number of British films produced in the UK fell 40% in 2004, from 45 in 2003 to 27 last year.
- The UK Film Council attributes the drop partly to 2003 being a strong year for British film production.
- The strength of the pound against the dollar is expected to have led to a decline in US films shot in the UK.
- The government closed tax loopholes affecting film investors.
- The UK Film Council is planning to announce a replacement for the UK film tax relief scheme Section 48 in July.
- It is too early to predict if the downward trend will continue throughout 2005.
