- UK schools will teach lessons on music piracy and copyright issues
- The education pack aims to give children an understanding of copyright in the music industry
- The pack has been requested by over 1,600 secondary schools
- British Music Rights (BMR) worked with education experts to create the pack
- Music piracy costs the UK music industry millions each year
- Piracy has been blamed for a decline in CD sales
- The lessons aim to protect young people planning a music career from "unscrupulous" individuals
