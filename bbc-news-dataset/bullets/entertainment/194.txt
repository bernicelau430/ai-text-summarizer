- The number of British films produced in the UK fell 40% in 2004.
- 27 British films were made in the UK in 2004, compared to 45 in 2003.
- The UK Film Council said the drop was partly due to 2003 being a good year for British film production.
- The strength of the pound against the dollar may have caused a fall in US films shot in the UK.
- The government closed tax loopholes used by film investors.
- The UK Film Council will announce a replacement for UK film tax relief scheme Section 48 in July.
- The reduction in indigenous film production is due to the trend towards co-production and changes in financing arrangements.
- It is "too early" to predict whether the downward trend will continue throughout 2005.
